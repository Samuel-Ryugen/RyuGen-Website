03 — The Technology Stack

One integrated stack. Three layers no competitor can replicate quickly.

Our architecture bridges high-performance computing and industrial energy efficiency — purpose-built hardware, patent-pending thermal recovery, and an embedded control plane in one sub-megawatt deployment that delivers compute and recovers its own heat.

Hardware

Helix Node

Modular, high-density compute built on NVIDIA-Certified systems — 16 NVIDIA RTX PRO 6000 Blackwell GPUs per node, direct liquid-to-chip cooling, cold-climate optimized to −30 °C — engineered to drop into existing commercial space and deliver enterprise-grade inference without hyperscale overhead.

Thermal IP · Provisional Patent

HeatSync™

Thermal architecture that doesn't just manage heat — it monetizes it. Our patent-pending system captures 90%+ of GPU waste heat and feeds a stable 60–70 °C loop into building thermal systems and industrial processes, turning a cooling cost into a revenue stream.

Software

RyuGen Command

The software engine behind the fleet: multi-objective workload scheduling, predictive thermal control, and behind-the-meter power balancing across every node we operate — for maximum uptime, efficiency, and grid harmony.

05 — The MoatHeatSync™

Every kilowatt of compute heat does two jobs.

Conventional liquid cooling rejects GPU heat to the environment. Helix runs hot on purpose — a 70 °C return loop that's warm enough to drive real building thermal systems. HeatSync buffers it into a stable source of year-round value.

The 70 °C return clears the adsorption-cooling threshold by 12 °C — opening direct hydronic heating, domestic hot-water pre-heat, and refrigerant-free cooling, all from the same loop. That's a thermal revenue stack and carbon-credit attribution no heat-rejecting competitor can claim.
